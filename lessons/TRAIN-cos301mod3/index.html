Section 1

What is a Tree?

After this part, you will know what a tree is and why it matters.

🌱 Watch This First

Press play to see how a farming family is like a tree.

A tree is a family structure

Think about your farm. There is a head farmer — the person who started it all. That farmer taught two or three people. Each of those people taught others. This makes a family tree shape.

🌾 Farming analogy: The head farmer sits at the top. Below him are the people he trained. Below them are the people they trained. Every person connects to the person above them — like branches on a real tree, but upside down.

In the computer world, a tree data structure works the same way. It is a way to store data (information) in a shape where things are connected from top to bottom.

Each piece of data sits in a node (a small box). Nodes connect to other nodes below them through links (like paths between farm plots).

⚽ Football analogy: Think of your team lineup. The coach is at the top. Under the coach are the captain and vice-captain. Under them are the defenders, midfielders, and strikers. Each level has a clear position, and everyone connects to someone above them.
💡 A tree is not a straight line like a queue. It branches out — one node can connect to many nodes below it.

Section 2

Key Words You Need

After this part, you will know all the important tree words.

The parts of a tree

Every tree has special parts. Let us learn each one.

1. Root

The root is the very first node at the top. There is only one root in a tree.

🌾 The root is like the head farmer — the one who started the whole farm. Every other person traces back to him.

2. Node

A node is any single item in the tree. It holds data (a value, a name, a number).

⚽ Each player on the team is a node. Each one has a name and a position.

3. Parent

A parent is a node that has nodes below it. The node above you is your parent.

🌾 The farmer who trained you is your parent. You learned from them.

4. Child

A child is a node below a parent. A parent can have many children.

⚽ If the captain passes to two midfielders, those midfielders are the captain's children in the tree.

5. Leaf

A leaf is a node with no children. It is at the bottom. Nothing grows below it.

🌾 A leaf is like a farmer who has no apprentice — no one is learning under them yet.

6. Path

A path is the route you take from one node to another, following the links.

⚽ The path is the passing chain — goalkeeper to defender to midfielder. Each pass is one step on the path.

7. Subtree

A subtree is a smaller tree inside the big tree. Pick any node — that node and everything below it is a subtree.

🌾 If one senior farmer has 3 workers under him, that group is a subtree — a small farm team inside the bigger farm.

8. Level

The level tells you how deep a node is. The root is at level 0. Its children are at level 1. Their children are at level 2, and so on.

⚽ Level 0 is the goalkeeper. Level 1 is the defenders. Level 2 is the midfielders. Level 3 is the strikers. Each line is one level deeper.

Types of Trees

1. General Tree

A general tree is the simplest kind. Each node can have any number of children. There is no limit.

🌾 This is like a farm where each farmer can train as many workers as they want. One farmer might train 2 people. Another might train 5. There is no rule about the number.

Node 2 has 3 children (5, 6, 7). Node 3 and 4 have none. No limit on children.

2. Binary Tree

A binary tree is a tree where each node can have at most 2 children. We call them the left child and the right child.

⚽ Think of it this way: each player can only pass to two people — one on the left and one on the right. Never more than two.

Every node has at most 2 children: left and right. No node has 3 or more.

3. Binary Search Tree (BST)

A binary search tree is a special binary tree with one important rule:

  • Numbers smaller than a node go to the left.
  • Numbers bigger than a node go to the right.

This rule makes it very fast to find any number.

🌾 Imagine you are sorting yams by size. You put smaller yams in the left basket and bigger yams in the right basket. Then for each basket, you sort again: smaller goes left, bigger goes right. This way, you can find any yam quickly — just keep asking "is it smaller or bigger?" at each step.

9 and 13 are less than 20, so they are on the left side. 21 and 30 are more than 20, so they are on the right.

What is Traversal?

Traversal means visiting every node in the tree, one by one. You might do this to search for something, or to print all the values.

⚽ Imagine the coach needs to check every player after a match — check their health, one by one. The order the coach visits them matters. Does he start with himself first? Or the left wing first?

There are three main ways to traverse a tree:

1. In-Order Traversal

The rule: Left → Root → Right

First visit everything on the left. Then visit the current node. Then visit everything on the right.

🌾 Imagine you check farms from left to right. You go to the leftmost farm plot first, check it. Then you come back to the farmer in charge, check him. Then you go to the right farm plot. Always: left field, then boss, then right field.

2. Pre-Order Traversal

The rule: Root → Left → Right

First visit the current node. Then go left. Then go right.

⚽ The coach checks himself first, then goes to check the left wing players, then the right wing players. The boss always comes first — that is why it is called pre-order (pre means before).

3. Post-Order Traversal

The rule: Left → Right → Root

First go left. Then go right. Visit the current node last.

🌾 The head farmer checks the left workers first, then the right workers, and only after everyone else is checked, he checks himself last. The boss comes last — that is post-order (post means after).

📋 Summary

MethodOrderEasy way to remember
In-OrderLeft → Root → RightBoss is IN the middle
Pre-OrderRoot → Left → RightBoss comes BEFORE (pre)
Post-OrderLeft → Right → RootBoss comes AFTER (post)
